Modeling Kedah-Kelantan (KK) Cattle Weight Trajectories

Field: Livestock Growth Kinetics

Tool: Calculus Derivatives $f'(x)$

The Bioscience Scenario

In Malaysian beef production, managing the growth efficiency of indigenous Kedah-Kelantan (KK) cattle is vital. Since feed translates directly to production costs, animal scientists must identify the exact period where the animal exhibits its maximum growth rate—the inflection point.

Using a sigmoidal growth model, we can map the total weight over time ($f(x)$) and apply the first derivative ($f'(x)$) to dynamically find the Average Daily Gain (ADG) at any given week of life.

Mathematical Model

Growth Function, $f(x)$:
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$
First Derivative (ADG), $f'(x)$ :
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$
Parameters (Realistic Malaysian KK Data):
  • $L = 220 \text{ kg}$ (Asymptotic Mature Weight)
  • $k = 0.08$ (Growth rate coefficient)
  • $x_0 = 35 \text{ weeks}$ (Time of maximum growth)
  • $x =$ Age of the cattle in weeks
Biological Benchmarks & Methodology Inspired By:
  • Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute (MARDI) publications on KK growth profiles.
  • Growth parameters adapted from standard Logistic kinetics observed in local pastured beef operations.
